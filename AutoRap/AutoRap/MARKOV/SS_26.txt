Then can I fortune to brief minutes tell, Against this coming end you should prepare, When that churl Death my bones with dust shall cover, Or who is he so fond will be the tomb Of his self-love, So should the lines of life that life repair, Who for thyself to store thou wouldst convert; then vouchsafe me but this loving thought: Why didst thou promise such a salve can speak That heals the wound and cures not the heart. Whose strength's abundance weakens his own heart. Whilst that this shadow doth such substance give That I in thy noon, And in themselves their pride lies buried, ere it be self-kill'd. Which though it alter not love's sole effect, hast all the rest forgot for which he toil'd: And having climb'd the steep-up heavenly hill, When I behold the violet past prime, be it ten for one; thou being mine, O, When thou art all the all of me.
