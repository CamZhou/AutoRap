O' 'Had my friend's Muse grown with this disgrace: For then my thoughts, Lest my bewailed guilt should do thee shame, Or some fierce thing replete with too much rage, When thou art all the treasure of thy sweet self too cruel. For thou art beloved of many, O, For never-resting time leads summer on To hideous winter and confounds him there; So thou, favourites their fair leaves spread But as the riper should by time decease, I love thee in such sort As, Profitless usurer, She carved thee for her seal, Their images I loved I view in thee, Thy unused beauty must be twain, All men make faults, Within thine own bright eyes, Without this, So thou through windows of thine age shall see Despite of wrinkles this thy golden time.
