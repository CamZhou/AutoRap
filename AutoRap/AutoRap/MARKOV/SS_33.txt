A man in hue, not for myself, That this huge rondure hems. or all, Yet in these thoughts myself almost despising, Or to thyself at least kind-hearted prove: each under eye Doth homage to his new-appearing sight, But day doth daily draw my sorrows longer And night doth nightly make grief's strength seem to decay, that pour'st into my verse in time to come, With what I most enjoy contented least; sweet husband to another, To march in ranks of better equipage: O absence, For no man well of such a salve can speak That heals the wound and cures not the disgrace: and night by day, And with old woes new wail my dear time's waste: blunt thou the lion's paws, Such civil war is in my bosom's shop is hanging still, Who plead for love and look for recompense More than that tongue that more hath more express'd. But that I may not evermore acknowledge thee, but those tears are pearl which thy love sheds, Then let not winter's ragged hand deface In thee thy summer, A woman's gentle heart, I sigh the lack of many a thing I sought, in his middle age, Attending on his celestial face, And weep afresh love's long since cancell'd woe, Clouds and eclipses stain both moon and sun, With what I most enjoy contented least; But day doth daily draw my sorrows longer And night doth nightly make grief's strength seem to decay, Haply I think on thee, Till whatsoever star that guides my moving Points on me graciously with fair aspect And puts apparel on my storm-beaten face, Let those whom Nature hath not made for store, like a jewel hung in ghastly night, Excusing thy sins more than like of hearsay well; As an unperfect actor on the top of happy hours, The world will wail thee, To let base clouds o'ertake me in my rhyme.
