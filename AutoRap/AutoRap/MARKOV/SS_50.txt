And then believe me, Desiring this man's art and that man's scope, Let me confess that we two must be twain, Desiring this man's art and that man's scope, Beauty's effect with beauty were bereft, Now stand you on the stage Who with his fair doth rehearse Making a famine where abundance lies, Clouds and eclipses stain both moon and sun, All losses are restored and sorrows end. Then of thy sweet love remember'd such wealth brings That then I scorn to change my mind! drawn by your own sweet argument, lives th' and thine for me Are windows to my breast, or wealth, Then, Then may I sing, Make sweet some vial; To thee I send this written embassage, Dear my love, none but unthrifts!
